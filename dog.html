" The Chihuahua "

The Chihuahua is a small breed of dog known for its tiny size, alert expression, and lively personality. Here is some information about Chihuahuas:

Size: Chihuahuas are one of the smallest dog breeds, typically weighing between 2 and 6 pounds (0.9 to 2.7 kilograms). They stand about 6 to 9 inches (15 to 23 centimeters) tall at the shoulder. There are two varieties of Chihuahuas based on coat length: smooth coat (short-haired) and long coat.

Origin: Chihuahuas get their name from the Mexican state of Chihuahua, where they were first discovered. They are believed to be descendants of the Techichi, a companion dog kept by the ancient Toltec civilization. Chihuahuas were brought to the United States in the mid-19th century and have since become a popular breed worldwide.

Temperament: Chihuahuas are known for their big personalities despite their small size. They are often confident, alert, and energetic dogs. They can be loyal and affectionate with their owners and tend to form strong bonds. Chihuahuas may be wary of strangers and can display protective behavior. Early socialization and training are important to ensure they are well-rounded and well-behaved dogs.

Care: Due to their small size, Chihuahuas have relatively low exercise needs. Regular walks and playtime are essential to keep them physically and mentally stimulated. Chihuahuas are prone to dental issues, so regular teeth brushing is recommended. They are also sensitive to cold weather, so providing them with appropriate clothing or a warm shelter is important.

Health: Chihuahuas generally have good overall health. However, they can be prone to certain health issues, including dental problems, heart conditions, patellar luxation (knee dislocation), and eye problems such as progressive retinal atrophy (PRA). Regular veterinary check-ups, a balanced diet, and proper dental care are important for maintaining their health.

Chihuahuas can make delightful companions for those who appreciate their lively and spirited nature. They are well-suited for individuals or families living in apartments or small homes. Proper care, training, and socialization are key to raising a happy and well-adjusted Chihuahua.

"The Pekingese "

The Pekingese is a small and distinctive toy breed of dog known for its regal appearance, luxurious coat, and independent nature. Here is some information about Pekingese dogs:

Appearance: Pekingese dogs have a compact and sturdy build with a distinctive lion-like appearance. They have a broad, flat face with a short muzzle, deep-set dark eyes, and a mane-like ruff of fur around their neck. Pekingese have a double coat, with a long, straight outer coat and a thick, dense undercoat. They come in various coat colors, including fawn, black, cream, and white.

Origin: The Pekingese breed has a rich history dating back over 2,000 years in China. They were highly regarded as companions and were kept by the Chinese imperial family. According to legend, they were created by Buddha, who shrunk down a lion to create this small and noble breed.

Temperament: Pekingese dogs are known for their independent and self-assured nature. They can be somewhat aloof with strangers but are generally devoted and loyal to their families. Pekingese dogs are often described as dignified, confident, and courageous. Despite their small size, they can be fearless and will not hesitate to defend their loved ones if they sense a threat.

Exercise and Care: Pekingese dogs are not highly energetic, but they still require daily exercise to maintain a healthy weight and mental stimulation. Short walks and playtime indoors are usually sufficient. They are sensitive to heat due to their flat faces, so care should be taken to avoid overheating in hot weather. Pekingese have a long, dense coat that requires regular grooming to prevent matting. Daily brushing, especially around the ears and tail, is recommended. They may also need periodic professional grooming.

Health: Pekingese dogs are generally healthy but can be prone to certain health issues. Due to their short muzzles, they can be susceptible to respiratory problems, eye issues, dental problems, and heat intolerance. Regular veterinary check-ups, a balanced diet, and maintaining a healthy weight are important for their well-being.

Pekingese dogs make excellent companions for individuals or families who appreciate their independent spirit and regal charm. They are best suited for owners who understand their specific needs and are willing to provide them with the care, grooming, and love they deserve.

Reference price
3,000,000 - 5,000,000 VND/head (Beijing purebred)

" The Dachshunds "

The Dachshund, also known as the "Wiener dog" or "sausage dog," is a small to medium-sized breed of dog known for its long body and short legs. Here is some information about Dachshunds:

Appearance: Dachshunds have a distinctive appearance with a long body, short legs, and a deep chest. They come in three coat varieties: smooth-haired, wire-haired, and long-haired. Dachshunds can have a wide range of coat colors and patterns, including solid colors, dappled, brindle, and piebald.

Origin: Dachshunds originated in Germany several hundred years ago. They were originally bred for hunting small game, such as badgers, hence their name, which means "badger dog" in German. Their long, low bodies and tenacious nature allowed them to dig into burrows and chase game.

Temperament: Dachshunds are known for their bold, curious, and independent nature. They are often described as lively, courageous, and spirited dogs. Despite their small size, they possess a strong hunting instinct and can be quite determined and stubborn at times. Dachshunds can be loving and loyal companions, but they may also have a strong personality and may require consistent training and socialization.

Health: Dachshunds are generally a healthy breed, but they can be prone to certain health issues. Due to their long spine, they are at a higher risk of developing intervertebral disc disease (IVDD). Obesity can also put strain on their backs, so maintaining a healthy weight is crucial. Regular exercise and a balanced diet are important for their overall well-being. Additionally, some Dachshunds may be predisposed to certain eye conditions, hip dysplasia, and dental problems.

Dachshunds are energetic and intelligent dogs that require mental and physical stimulation. They can adapt well to different living environments, including apartments, as long as they receive regular exercise and mental enrichment. Proper training, socialization, and regular veterinary care are important to ensure they lead a healthy and happy life.

" The Corgi "

The Corgi is a small to medium-sized herding dog breed that is known for its short legs, long body, and expressive face. Here is some information about Corgis:

Breed Varieties: There are two main breeds of Corgis: the Pembroke Welsh Corgi and the Cardigan Welsh Corgi. While they share some similarities, they have distinct characteristics and origins.

Pembroke Welsh Corgi: The Pembroke Welsh Corgi is the more popular of the two Corgi breeds. They have a fox-like face, erect ears, and a docked or naturally bobbed tail. Pembrokes have a medium-length, double-layered coat that comes in various colors, with red and white being the most common.

Cardigan Welsh Corgi: The Cardigan Welsh Corgi is the older of the two Corgi breeds. They have a more substantial build, with a long tail, rounded ears, and a foxy expression. Cardigans have a long, double-layered coat that comes in a range of colors, including brindle, sable, and blue merle.

History: Corgis have a rich history as herding dogs originating from Wales, United Kingdom. They were primarily bred for their herding abilities, particularly with cattle. Corgis are known for their agility, intelligence, and strong work ethic.

Temperament: Corgis are intelligent, alert, and affectionate dogs. They are known for their big personalities and playful nature. Corgis are generally good with families and are often good with children and other pets if properly socialized. They can have a strong herding instinct, which may lead them to try herding people or other animals.

Exercise and Training: Despite their short legs, Corgis are energetic dogs that require regular exercise to maintain their physical and mental well-being. Daily walks, playtime, and mental stimulation are important for keeping them happy and healthy. Corgis are generally eager to please and respond well to positive reinforcement-based training methods.

Health: Like all dog breeds, Corgis can be prone to certain health conditions. Some common health issues seen in Corgis include hip dysplasia, degenerative myelopathy, progressive retinal atrophy, and obesity. Regular veterinary check-ups, a balanced diet, and appropriate exercise are important for their overall health.

Corgis are delightful and loyal companions known for their distinctive appearance and spirited personality. They require proper training, socialization, and regular exercise to thrive in a loving home environment.

Reference price: $800 to $2,500

" The Poodle "

The Poodle is a highly intelligent and versatile breed of dog known for its distinctive curly or corded coat. Here is some information about Poodles:

Coat Types: Poodles have a single-layered, dense, and curly coat that is hypoallergenic and minimally sheds. The coat can be groomed in various styles, including the well-known "poodle clip" with shaved body areas and pom-poms on the tail and legs. Some Poodles have corded coats, which form long, rope-like cords if left to naturally grow and mat.

Intelligence and Trainability: Poodles are widely regarded as one of the most intelligent dog breeds. They are highly trainable, responsive, and eager to please their owners. Poodles excel in obedience training, agility, and other dog sports. Their intelligence and versatility make them excellent working dogs and companions.

Temperament: Poodles are known for their friendly, active, and alert nature. They are typically good with children and other pets when properly socialized. Poodles are often described as being loyal, trainable, and playful. They thrive on human companionship and may suffer from separation anxiety if left alone for extended periods.

Energy Level and Exercise Needs: Poodles are energetic dogs that require regular exercise and mental stimulation. Daily walks, playtime, and interactive activities are important to keep them physically and mentally stimulated. They adapt well to different lifestyles, from active individuals or families to more relaxed environments, as long as they receive their required exercise.

Health: Poodles are generally a healthy breed with a long lifespan. However, like all dog breeds, they can be prone to certain health conditions. These may include hip dysplasia, progressive retinal atrophy (PRA), epilepsy, allergies, and certain genetic disorders. Responsible breeders perform health screenings and genetic tests to minimize the risk of hereditary diseases.

Poodles require regular grooming to maintain their coat's health and prevent matting. Many Poodle owners choose to have their dogs professionally groomed every few weeks. Regular brushing, ear cleaning, dental care, and nail trimming are also important parts of their grooming routine.

As with any dog breed, responsible ownership, proper socialization, regular veterinary care, and a balanced diet are essential for the well-being of Poodles.

Reference price: 6,000,000 – 10,000,000 VND/head

" The Pug "

The Pug is a small breed of dog known for its wrinkled face, short muzzle, and charming personality. Here is some information about Pugs:

Appearance: Pugs have a distinctive appearance with a round head, large expressive eyes, and deep wrinkles on their forehead. They have a short, smooth coat that comes in various colors, including fawn, black, and silver. Pugs have a compact, muscular body with a curled tail that rests tightly over their back.

Personality: Pugs have a delightful and comical personality. They are known for their clownish behavior and love to entertain their owners with their antics. Pugs are generally happy and adaptable dogs, able to adapt to various living situations, from apartments to larger homes.

Health: Pugs are brachycephalic dogs, meaning they have a short muzzle and a flattened face. This facial structure can lead to certain health issues, including breathing difficulties, eye problems, dental issues, and overheating in hot weather. Pugs are also prone to obesity, so a balanced diet and regular exercise are crucial to maintain their weight and overall health.

Exercise and Activity Level: Pugs have a moderate exercise requirement and enjoy regular walks and playtime. However, it's important not to overexert them due to their brachycephalic nature. Pugs are not typically high-energy dogs and are generally content with moderate activity levels.

Grooming: Pugs have a short, easy-to-maintain coat. They shed moderately, so regular brushing helps to remove loose hair and keep their coat in good condition. Their facial wrinkles require special attention to keep them clean and prevent skin issues. Routine care includes cleaning their wrinkles, trimming nails, and regular dental hygiene.

It's important to note that Pugs may require special care and attention due to their brachycephalic characteristics. When looking for a Pug, it is recommended to choose a responsible breeder who prioritizes the health and well-being of their dogs. Regular veterinary check-ups and a watchful eye on their weight and respiratory health are essential for ensuring the happiness and longevity of Pugs.

" The Alaskan Malamute "

The Alaskan Malamute is a large breed of domestic dog that was originally bred for hauling heavy freight and sledges over long distances in the harsh Arctic climate of Alaska. Here are some key characteristics and information about the breed:

Appearance: Alaskan Malamutes are a powerful, sturdy, and heavy-boned breed with a thick, double-layered coat that is designed to protect them from the cold. They have a broad, wolf-like head with almond-shaped eyes that can be brown or blue.

Size: Alaskan Malamutes are considered a large breed of dog, typically weighing between 75 to 100 pounds and standing between 22 and 26 inches tall at the shoulder.

Personality: Alaskan Malamutes are known for being friendly, loyal, and affectionate dogs. They are highly intelligent, but can be independent and stubborn at times. They are a very social breed and do well in homes where they have regular interaction with their human family.

Exercise and Training: Alaskan Malamutes are active and energetic dogs that require a lot of exercise and mental stimulation to stay healthy and happy. They were bred for endurance and strength, so they need regular exercise such as long walks, hikes, or runs. They also require consistent training and socialization to ensure they are well-behaved and obedient.

Health: Like all dog breeds, Alaskan Malamutes can be prone to certain health issues such as hip dysplasia, bloat, and eye problems. It's important to buy from a reputable breeder who tests their dogs for potential genetic health issues.

Alaskan Malamutes can make wonderful pets for the right owner, but they require a lot of space, attention, and care. They do well in homes with large yards or access to outdoor space, and need regular grooming to maintain their thick coat. They are not recommended for apartment living.

" The Husky "

The term "Husky" commonly refers to the Siberian Husky, a breed of medium-sized working dog that originated in northeastern Siberia. Here is some information about the Siberian Husky:

Appearance: Siberian Huskies are known for their striking appearance. They have a strong and athletic build with a thick double coat that helps them withstand cold weather. They come in various colors and patterns, including black, white, gray, red, and sable. Their eyes are typically blue or a combination of blue and brown.

Size: Siberian Huskies are considered a medium-sized breed. They typically weigh between 35 to 60 pounds (16 to 27 kilograms) and stand around 20 to 24 inches (51 to 61 centimeters) tall at the shoulder.

Temperament: Huskies are friendly, outgoing, and sociable dogs. They are known for their playful and mischievous nature. They are pack-oriented and get along well with other dogs and even some other animals. Huskies are generally good with children, but supervision is always recommended. They are not typically aggressive towards humans.

Energy and Exercise: Siberian Huskies are highly energetic and require regular exercise and mental stimulation to be happy and well-behaved. They have a strong instinct to run and explore, so they benefit from activities such as running, hiking, or participating in dog sports like mushing, skijoring, or canicross. A securely fenced yard is recommended, as Huskies have a tendency to wander and may not be reliable off-leash.

Health: Siberian Huskies are generally a healthy breed, but they can be prone to certain health issues, including hip dysplasia, eye problems such as cataracts or progressive retinal atrophy (PRA), and certain genetic conditions like zinc deficiency (zinc-responsive dermatosis). Regular veterinary check-ups, a balanced diet, and appropriate exercise are essential for their overall well-being.

Grooming: Huskies have a thick double coat that requires regular brushing to maintain its health and minimize shedding. They have a natural ability to clean themselves and are not prone to a strong "doggy" odor. However, they do shed heavily twice a year during shedding seasons, known as "blowing their coat."

Siberian Huskies are intelligent, beautiful, and active dogs that thrive in environments that allow them to engage in physical and mental activities. They require a committed and experienced owner who can provide them with the exercise, training, and socialization they need to be happy and well-adjusted companions.

Reference price: 6,000,000 - 10,000,000 VND/head (Husky imported in other countries with documents will be more expensive)

" The Pomeranian "

The Pomeranian is a small breed of dog known for its fluffy coat, charming personality, and vibrant energy. Here is some information about the Pomeranian:

Appearance: Pomeranians are small dogs with a compact body and a luxurious double coat. They have a fox-like face with a short muzzle and bright, almond-shaped eyes. Pomeranians come in a wide variety of colors, including orange, black, white, cream, sable, and more. They have a plumed tail that curls over their back.

Size: Pomeranians are classified as a toy breed and are one of the smallest dog breeds. They typically weigh between 3 to 7 pounds (1.4 to 3.2 kilograms) and stand around 6 to 7 inches (15 to 18 centimeters) tall at the shoulder.

Temperament: Pomeranians are known for their confident, extroverted, and lively personality. They are often described as being curious, intelligent, and eager to please. Pomeranians tend to bond closely with their owners and can be quite protective of them. They may be wary of strangers but can warm up with proper socialization.

Energy Level and Exercise: Despite their small size, Pomeranians are energetic dogs and require regular exercise to stay happy and healthy. Daily walks, interactive play sessions, and mental stimulation are important for their well-being. However, their exercise needs can be met within the home or a small yard, making them suitable for apartment living.

Health: Pomeranians are generally healthy dogs but, like all breeds, they may be prone to certain health issues. Some common health concerns in Pomeranians include dental problems, luxating patellas (loose kneecaps), collapsing trachea, and certain eye conditions. Regular veterinary check-ups and a balanced diet are essential for their overall health and well-being.

Pomeranians are delightful companion dogs, well-suited for families, individuals, and seniors alike. Their small size, friendly nature, and glamorous appearance make them popular pets. However, it's important to remember that they require proper care, attention, and socialization to thrive as beloved family members.
