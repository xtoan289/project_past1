Origin and history: The Arabian Mau is believed to be one of the oldest cat breeds in the world, with a lineage dating back thousands of years.

"The Persian cat"

The Persian cat is a popular and well-known breed of domestic cat that is known for its luxurious long coat and distinctive facial features. Here is some information about Persian cats:

Appearance: Persian cats have a round face with a short nose, large round eyes, and small ears. Their most prominent feature is their long, thick coat, which requires regular grooming to prevent matting. They come in a wide variety of colors and patterns, including solid, shaded, smoke, tabby, and bicolor.

History: Persian cats are one of the oldest and most recognizable cat breeds. They originated in Persia (now Iran) and were introduced to Europe in the 17th century. Over time, selective breeding has led to the development of the Persian's unique appearance.

Temperament: Persian cats are known for their calm and gentle nature. They are typically quiet and enjoy a relaxed lifestyle. They are affectionate towards their owners and often enjoy being lap cats. Persians are generally not as active or playful as some other breeds, preferring a peaceful and comfortable environment.

Care: Due to their long coat, Persian cats require regular grooming to keep their fur in good condition. Daily brushing is recommended to prevent tangles and matting. Additionally, their eyes should be regularly cleaned to prevent tear staining. Persians should be kept indoors to protect their coat from dirt, parasites, and potential injuries.

Health: Persian cats are prone to certain health issues, including polycystic kidney disease (PKD), hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM), and respiratory problems due to their short nose.

It's important to note that Persian cats require regular grooming and maintenance due to their long coat, and potential owners should be prepared to dedicate time and effort to their care.

Reference price: 22 – 70 million VND.

"The Sphynx cat"

The Sphynx cat is a unique and distinctive breed known for its lack of fur and wrinkled skin. Here is some information about Sphynx cats:

Appearance: The most notable feature of the Sphynx cat is its hairless or nearly hairless body. However, they are not completely bald and often have a thin layer of downy fuzz. Their skin may have various pigments and patterns, giving them a textured and wrinkled appearance. They have a muscular body, prominent cheekbones, and large ears.

Origin: The Sphynx breed originated in the 1960s in Canada when a natural genetic mutation caused a hairless kitten to be born. Through selective breeding, this unique trait was propagated, and the Sphynx breed was developed.

Temperament: Sphynx cats are known for their friendly, affectionate, and sociable nature. They are highly people-oriented and enjoy being the center of attention. Sphynx cats are often described as extroverted, playful, and curious. They thrive on human companionship and make excellent pets for those seeking an interactive and affectionate feline companion.

Care: Although Sphynx cats are hairless, they require regular maintenance. Their skin produces natural oils that need to be wiped away to prevent a greasy buildup. They may also need regular bathing to keep their skin clean. Additionally, since they lack fur, they are sensitive to temperature changes and may require extra warmth in colder climates.

Health: Sphynx cats are generally healthy, but they may have specific health considerations. Due to their hairless nature, they are more susceptible to sunburn, so it's important to protect their skin from excessive sunlight. They can also be prone to certain skin conditions and may require regular skin checks.

It's worth noting that despite their lack of fur, Sphynx cats still produce allergens and may not be hypoallergenic for individuals with cat allergies. It's advisable to spend time with a Sphynx cat before deciding to bring one into your home if you have allergies.

Reference price: 22 – 70 million VND.

"The British Longhair"

The British Longhair is a breed of domestic cat that is known for its plush, long coat and charming appearance. Here is some information about the British Longhair:

Appearance: The British Longhair is a medium to large-sized cat with a sturdy and muscular build. They have a round face, full cheeks, and round, expressive eyes. Their most distinctive feature is their long, dense coat that comes in various colors and patterns. The coat is soft, silky, and requires regular grooming to prevent matting.

Origin: The British Longhair is derived from the British Shorthair breed. Longhair kittens can occasionally occur in British Shorthair litters due to recessive genes. Breeders started selectively breeding these longhaired kittens to establish the British Longhair as a separate breed.

Temperament: British Longhairs are known for their calm and reserved nature. They tend to be gentle, easygoing, and well-mannered cats. They enjoy a relaxed lifestyle and are not as active or playful as some other breeds. British Longhairs are typically affectionate with their families but may be reserved with strangers.

Care: The British Longhair's coat requires regular grooming to prevent matting and to keep it in good condition. Daily brushing is recommended to remove loose hair and prevent tangles. They may also need occasional bathing to maintain the cleanliness of their long coat. Regular dental care and nail trimming are also essential for their overall well-being.

Health: British Longhairs are generally healthy cats with no specific breed-related health issues. However, like all cats, they should receive regular veterinary check-ups to monitor their health and address any concerns that may arise.

It's important to note that the British Longhair requires commitment to regular grooming and maintenance due to its long coat, and potential owners should be prepared to invest time and effort in their care.

"The Siamese cat"

The Siamese cat is a distinct and popular breed known for its striking blue almond-shaped eyes, color-pointed coat pattern, and vocal nature. Here is some information about Siamese cats:

Appearance: Siamese cats have a medium-sized, muscular body with a sleek and elegant appearance. They have a triangular-shaped head with a long, slender neck, and their eyes are a vibrant blue color. The most characteristic feature of Siamese cats is their color-pointed coat pattern, where the body is lighter in color while the face, ears, paws, and tail are darker.

Origin: Siamese cats are one of the oldest and most recognized cat breeds. They originated in Siam (now Thailand) and were initially kept as royal companions.

Temperament: Siamese cats are known for their intelligence, playfulness, and social nature. They are highly active and require mental and physical stimulation. Siamese cats are often described as being affectionate, loving, and demanding of attention. They are known to be quite vocal and will often "talk" to their owners with their unique, loud, and raspy voice. s

Care: Siamese cats have a short, fine coat that requires minimal grooming. Regular brushing helps to remove loose hair and maintain a healthy coat. Due to their high activity levels, providing them with interactive toys and playtime is important to keep them mentally and physically stimulated.

Health: Siamese cats are generally healthy, but they may be prone to certain genetic health issues. These can include dental problems, respiratory conditions (such as asthma), and certain eye conditions (such as progressive retinal atrophy).

Siamese cats make wonderful companions for those who appreciate their beauty, intelligence, and outgoing personality. They thrive in households with plenty of love, attention, and mental stimulation.

"The Ragdoll cat"

The Ragdoll cat is a popular and distinct breed known for its large size, gentle temperament, and tendency to go limp when picked up. Here is some information about Ragdoll cats:

Appearance: Ragdolls are large, muscular cats with a semi-longhaired coat. They have striking blue eyes, a broad head, and a well-developed chin. Their coat is silky and dense, and it comes in various colors and patterns, including colorpoint, mitted, and bicolor.

Temperament: Ragdolls are known for their docile and gentle nature. They are typically relaxed and enjoy being held or carried, often going limp in their owner's arms, hence the name "Ragdoll." They are sociable cats and often get along well with other pets and children. Ragdolls are not typically as active or playful as some other breeds but still enjoy interactive playtime and gentle companionship.

Origin: The Ragdoll breed was developed in the 1960s in California, United States, by a breeder named Ann Baker. The breed was created through selective breeding of cats with certain traits, including their gentle temperament and tendency to go limp when handled.

Care: Ragdolls have a semi-longhaired coat that requires regular grooming to prevent matting and keep it in good condition. Brushing a few times a week is usually sufficient. They shed moderately, with heavier shedding during seasonal changes.

Health: Ragdolls are generally considered a healthy breed. However, they can be prone to certain health issues, including hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM), which is a heart condition. Regular veterinary check-ups and a balanced diet are essential for their well-being.

Ragdolls make excellent pets for those seeking a gentle and affectionate companion. Their calm and easygoing nature, combined with their beautiful appearance, have earned them a special place in the hearts of cat lovers.

Reference price: 6-20 million VND

"The Tabby cat"

The term "tabby cat" refers to a coat pattern rather than a specific breed. Tabby cats are among the most common and recognizable domestic cats. Here is some information about tabby cats:

Coat Pattern: Tabby cats have a distinct coat pattern that consists of stripes, swirls, or spots. The pattern is often most visible on their body, tail, and limbs. The background color of the coat can vary, with tabby cats found in various shades of brown, gray, orange, and cream. The classic tabby pattern features bold, dark stripes that resemble the letter "M" on the forehead.

Tabby Markings: Tabby cats can display different types of tabby markings. These include classic, mackerel, spotted, and ticked. Classic tabbies have bold swirls or marbling patterns on their body. Mackerel tabbies have thin, parallel stripes running vertically along their body. Spotted tabbies have distinct spots instead of stripes. Ticked tabbies have fur with a subtle agouti pattern, giving the appearance of a solid coat from a distance.

Breeds: Tabby cats can be found in various breeds, including the domestic shorthair and domestic longhair. Tabby markings can be seen in both mixed-breed cats and specific cat breeds, such as the Maine Coon, American Shorthair, Bengal, and Abyssinian. Tabby patterns can also be present in cats with mixed ancestry.

Personality: Tabby cats come in various personalities as they are not defined by a specific breed. However, many tabby cats are known to be friendly, sociable, and outgoing. They can make loving and affectionate companions.

Popularity: Tabby cats are popular pets due to their unique and beautiful coat patterns. Their markings make them easily recognizable and sought after by cat lovers. They can be found in homes worldwide and have a special place in the hearts of many cat enthusiasts.

Remember, tabby cats are not a breed but a coat pattern that can be found in various cat breeds and mixed-breed cats. The tabby pattern is a natural and common variation in domestic cats, adding to their charm and individuality.

Reference price: 150k-200k VND
